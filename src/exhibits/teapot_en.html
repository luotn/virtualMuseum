Yaozhou Kiln Floral Carved Celadon Bottom-filling Pot (Replica)


Celadon from the Five Dynasties


Yaozhou Kiln Floral Carved Celadon Bottom-filling Pot was unearthed in Bin County, Shaanxi Province in 1968. Now in Shaanxi History Museum.


18.3cm tall and 14.3cm in diameter.
Has a phoenix-style handle and thumbpiece, a flower base as the "pot cover".
The joint between the lid and the pot is decorated with sculptures of a nursing lion mother and her cub, with the lioness opening her mouth as spout.
The spherical body is engraved with peonies.
There is a plum blossom-shaped water injection hole in the center of the bottom.
The combination of these shapes give this bottom-filling pot a distinctive apperance.


This pot unique in design and very creative. Its shape and structure are novel and unique, integrating practicality and artistry, which can be described as ingenious workmanship. On August 19, 2013, China Administration of Cultural Heritage listed it in the "Third Batch of Cultural Relics Prohibited from Exhibiting Abroad".
