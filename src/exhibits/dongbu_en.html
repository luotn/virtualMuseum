Ironware from Qing Dynasty


A vessel used by the Mongolian people to hold milk and tea. Dongbu teapot originates from the Domu teapot in Central Asia and has been popular in Mongolia since the Yuan Dynasty. The body of the vessel is narrow and wide at the bottom, and it is decorated with three copper hoops. The shape of this pot is simple and simple, generous and practical.
