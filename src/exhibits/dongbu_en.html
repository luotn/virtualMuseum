Dongbu Pot

Modern Mongolian Ethnic Tall Pot


Length / Height: 33cm, Maximum Width / Diameter: 12.5cm.

A vessel used by the Mongolian Ethnic people to hold milk and tea. Origionated from the Domu pot from the middle Asian. The Domu pot comes from the Mongolian and Tibetan ethnic areas and is generally used to hold butter tea. It is usually made of wood or metal. The Domu pot is called "Lemu" in Tibetan, "Dongbu pot" in Mongolian, and "Domu pot" in the central area. Porcelain Domu pots first appeared in the Yuan Dynasty and became popular again during the Kangxi period of the Qing Dynasty. In the Yuan Dynasty, Jingdezhen kilns produced blue and white and blue and white porcelain varieties. The Yuan Dynasty vessels were smaller in shape and had handles. Kangxi vessels are larger in shape and have no handles.

The body of the pot is narrow and wider at the bottom, and it is decorated with three copper hoops. The shape of this pot is simple, yet substantial and practical.
